Method

Dream the next state, then retrieve the action that likely caused it.

RAID is a compositional policy: a frozen world-model encoder hallucinates the latent successor of the current observation, while a memory-augmented inverse-dynamics head decodes the implied motor command by blending parametric and retrieval-based estimates.

Problem statement. Let ft denote a GR-1 vision-feature observation and at the action applied at step t. Given a memory bank M = {(fi, fi+1, ai)} of demonstrated transitions, the inverse-dynamics task is to predict a normalised action ât from (ft, ft+1).

At deployment, GR-1 supplies a one-step-ahead dreamed feature f̂t+1 that stands in for the unobserved next state. For a query transition we retrieve the top-k most similar memory entries by cosine similarity in the joint (ft, ft+1) space and compute a pooled prior âprior from the retrieved actions. A parametric trunk dφ(ft, ft+1) provides a complementary direct estimate and a per-dimension gate blends the two.

General formulation. Our formulation mirrors DreamZero. GR-1 + RAID makes a single substitution: the parametric inverse dynamics model is replaced by a retrieval-augmented decoder that conditions on k = 3 demonstrated transitions from M alongside the dreamed latent transition.

GR-1 + Cross-Attention RAID. We freeze the GR-1 encoder and use its 384-dimensional class-token feature as both the current state ft and (via GR-1's one-step prediction head) the dreamed next state f̂t+1. The RAID head sits on top with three components:

(i) Direct trunk. A two-hidden-layer MLP over concat(ft, ft+1); LayerNorm + ReLU + dropout 0.1.

(ii) Cross-attention prior. Project concat(ft, ft+1) to a query q ∈ ℝd and each retrieved action ai to a key ki ∈ ℝd:

αi = softmax(qTki / √d)     âprior = Σ αi · ai

The values are the actions themselves, so the prior remains in 7-D action space and stays interpretable.

(iii) Per-dimension gate. The trunk and prior are blended dimension-wise:

â = g ⊙ dφ(ft, ft+1) + (1 − g) ⊙ âprior
g = σ(W concat(ft, ft+1) + b)

At training time we apply dropout (p = 0.5) and additive Gaussian jitter (σ = 0.1) to the retrieved prior. Without this regularization the optimizer copies the prior unchanged; the dropout and noise force the trunk to do real work.

⊕

Direct Trunk

Two-hidden-layer MLP over concatenated features. Provides a parametric baseline that learns from all training data.

⚡

Cross-Attention Prior

Retrieves k=3 nearest transitions by cosine similarity and attends over their actions, producing an interpretable action template.

◫

Per-Dimension Gate

Sigmoid gate blends trunk and prior independently for each DOF, letting the model defer to retrieval where it helps most.

Architecture

The full RAID pipeline.

A frozen GR-1 encoder dreams the next latent observation. The RAID head maps (current, dreamed) features to a 7-DOF action by combining a parametric trunk with a cross-attention prior over retrieved demonstrator transitions.

Results

Retrieval gives the inverse-dynamics head a sharper action prior.

On LIBERO-Spatial with 25 demonstrations, cross-attention RAID over GR-1 features reaches 0.132 validation MSE versus 0.842 for the same visual head without retrieval — a 6.4× improvement.
